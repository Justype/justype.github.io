What do you think of Shylock?

He is the epitome of ethnic and religious conflicts.

  • At that time, the Jews would be severely oppressed. Due to the decree, they must conceal their identity and religious beliefs. Meanwhile, jews was not easy to keep wealth from government. So he had no choice but to live on usury.
  • Their different religious beliefs made big differences in thinking. Sherlock lived a temperate life. And he scorned Christian luxury. This is not just a conflict between individuals, but also between race and religion.

William Blake

  • Songs of Innocence, published in 1809, is a lovely volume of poems. The poems praise the beauty of nature and the innocence of the child.
  • In the poem “Songs of Innocence”, “lamb” is the symbol of innocence in Christianity.
  • Songs of Experience paints this world of misery, poverty, disease, war and repression with a melancholy tone.
  • In the line of the poem “The Chimney Sweeper”, “a heaven of our misery” is a figure of speech called oxymoron. “Heaven” and “misery”, two words of opposite meaning, are put together to create strong ironic effect.

Robert Burns

  • “A Red, Red Rose” is one of Burns most popular songs of love in Scottish dialect.
  • Robert Burns is most famous for his songs, in which he uses simple and musical language to celebrate love, friendship, patriotism and other beautiful human feelings and emotions.
