  • Sonnet 75 starting with the line “One day I wrote her name upon the strand” was written by Edmund Spenser.

  • Sir Philip Sidney is an Elizabethan poet who stood for the Renaissance ideal of “the complete man.”
  • The basic unit of poetic meter in English is a foot which normally consists of one stressed syllable plus one or two unstressed syllables.
  • A four-line stanza is called a quatrain.
  • Chaucer is often regarded as the father of English Literature.

  • Referential language refers to the language that is limited to everyday real life and situational use.
  • Representational language refers to the language that engages one’s emotion and imagination.

  • There are 10 syllables in a line and they follow a regular pattern: an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed one. This line is called a(an) iambic pentameter line.

  • Sonnet (literary form) is popular in Elizabethan age.
  • "Renaissance" means rebirth or revival of letters. It encourages a rebirth of human spirit. (Humanism)
