Week8

  • Written in 1837-38, Oliver Twist tells the story of an orphan boy, whose adventures provide material for a description of the lower depths of London.
  • Naturalism accepts the theory of “survival of the fittest” and believes in pessimistic determinism.
  • Realism is a literary trend in nineteenth century and it also refers to a way of writing which presents life as it really is.
  • Victorian dramatist and novelist Oscar Wilde believed that art had nothing to do with morality, but should exist for its own sake. Art was independent of life. Art was not the reflection of nature but rather nature was created by art.
  • Thomas Hardy believes that man’s fate is predetermined tragic, driven by a combined force of “nature”, both inside and outside.
  • Most of George Bernard Shaw’s plays are concerned with political, economic, moral, or religious problems, so they are often termed as problem plays.
  • Written by George Bernard Shaw in 1893, and first performed in London in 1902, Mrs. Warren's Profession is about a former prostitute, now a madam and brothel proprietor, who attempts to come to terms with her disapproving daughter, illustrating Shaw's belief that the act of prostitution was not caused by moral failure but by economic necessity.
  • Tess of the d'Urbervilles attracted criticism for its sympathetic portrayal of a "fallen woman" and was initially refused publication. Its subtitle, A Pure Woman: Faithfully Presented, was intended to raise the eyebrows of the Victorian middle classes.
  • In The Importance of Being Earnest, Wilde followed the tradition of the comedy of manners of the 18th century.
  • In Chapter 5 of A Tale of Two Cities, it describes the scene that a cask of wine is broke in the street. People rush in to drink every drop of it. In order not to waste the wine, they even take away the mud together with the wine. They celebrate it as if it were a holiday.
  • Pygmalion, one of George Bernard Shaw's most famous plays, was probably his most popular play.
  • The Return of the Native is written by Thomas Hardy.
  • Vanity Fair is the most successful novel by Thackeray, which follows the lives of Becky Sharp and Amelia Sedley amid their friends and families during and after the Napoleonic Wars.
  • A Tale of Two Cities has a famous opening sentence: “It was the best of times, it was the worst of times, it was the age of wisdom, it was the age of foolishness, it was the epoch of belief, it was the epoch of incredulity, it was the season of Light, it was the season of Darkness, it was the spring of hope, it was the winter of despair, we had everything before us, we had nothing before us, we were all going direct to Heaven, we were all going direct the other way…”
